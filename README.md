📌 Project Overview

The IoT & AI-Enabled Skill Training Equipment Monitoring System is an intelligent web-based platform designed to monitor, manage, and optimize the usage of skill training equipment in educational institutions, training centers, laboratories, and industrial workshops.

The system integrates Internet of Things (IoT) devices with Artificial Intelligence (AI) to provide real-time equipment monitoring, predictive maintenance, performance analytics, and smart decision-making. IoT sensors continuously collect operational data such as power consumption, temperature, vibration, runtime, and equipment status. AI algorithms analyze this data to identify abnormal behavior, predict equipment failures, and recommend maintenance actions before breakdowns occur.

The platform helps administrators, trainers, and students improve equipment utilization, reduce maintenance costs, prevent unexpected downtime, and create a more efficient learning environment.

🎯 Problem Statement

Skill development centers and engineering laboratories often manage a large number of expensive training equipment. Equipment failures, improper maintenance, and inefficient utilization reduce training quality and increase operational costs.

Traditional maintenance methods depend on manual inspections, making it difficult to identify problems before equipment failures occur.

There is a need for an intelligent monitoring system capable of continuously tracking equipment conditions, predicting failures, and providing actionable insights to improve operational efficiency.

💡 Solution

This project provides an integrated IoT and AI solution that continuously monitors training equipment through connected sensors.

The collected sensor data is transmitted to the cloud, where AI models analyze equipment behavior and predict possible failures. Administrators receive instant alerts and maintenance recommendations through an interactive dashboard.

The system improves equipment availability, enhances safety, and ensures uninterrupted practical training.
